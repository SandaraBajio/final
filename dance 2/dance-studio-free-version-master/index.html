Types of DANCES

Types of Dances

Ballet

Ballet is an artistic dance form performed to music using precise and highly formalized set steps and gestures. Classical ballet, which originated in Renaissance Italy and established its present form during the 19th century, is characterized by light, graceful fluid movements and the use of pointe shoes. It is performed on a stage to an audience utilizing costumes, scenic design and lighting. It can tell a story or express a thought, concept or emotion. Ballet dance can be magical, exciting, provoking or disturbing.

Hip Hop

Hip hop dance is a range of street dance styles primarily performed to hip hop music or that have evolved as part of hip hop culture. It is a vibrant form of dance that combines a variety of freestyle movements to create a cultural piece of art. It is influenced by a wide range of styles that were created in the 1970s and made popular by dance crews in the United States.Through its three main styles of popping, locking, and breaking, hip-hop dance has evolved into one of the most popular and influential styles of dance.

Folk Dance

Folk Dance a type of dance that is a vernacular, usually recreational, expression of a past or present culture. It represents the lives of people from a specific region or country. They are often performed at cultural gatherings, festivals, or agricultural events. Folk dances are usually performed by nonprofessionals and accompanied by traditional folk music.It serves as a form of storytelling and preservation of the country 's past , as well as a way to celebrate and honor its cultural identity

Contemporary

Contemporary is one of the most expressive forms of dance, contemporary allows you to create a story through cultivating forms of movement. Contemporary can be understood as an amalgamation of influences; those most notably referenced to modern and ballet. The story goes that a number of dancers and choreographers broke free from the traditional styles of ballet and performance and wanted to be taken more seriously as artists rather than entertainers.

Ballroom

Ballroom is a partnership dance where couples, using step-patterns, move rhythmically, expressing the characteristics of music. It is a popular style of partner dance that includes several distinct types such as Waltz, Tango, Foxtrot, Quick Step and Cha-cha, each with its own set of techniques, rhythms, and cultural backgrounds.

History of DANCE

HISTORY

Dance has always been with us, even before the advent of written language and contemporary history. Our earliest cultures formed using oral and performance means to pass down stories from one generation to the next. Many historians believe that social, celebratory, and ritual dances played an important role in the development of early human civilization. Dance has been associated with ancient rites, spiritual meetings, and social activities since the beginning of recorded history. Dance, as a conduit of trance, spiritual force, pleasure, expression, performance, and contact, became ingrained in our nature from the moment the first African tribes covered themselves in war-paint to the expansion of and dance over the world. Without a question, dancing remains one of the most expressive forms of communication that we know. The very first physical proof to support the question of where does dance come from are 9,000 year old cave paintings in India. Imagery of humans socially gathering and dancing were found alongside carved stories of hunting, childbirth, rituals, and burials.

Famous DANCERS Around the World

Anna Pavlova(1881-1931) Anna Pavlova was one of the most celebrated and influential ballerinas of the early 20th century. Pavlova's delicate and emotive style set a new standard for ballet performance. Her interpretation of roles emphasized expressive, lyrical qualities, influencing future generations of dancers. Her legacy endures through the numerous films, biographies, and artworks dedicated to her life and career. Her portrayal of "The Dying Swan" remains one of the most iconic images in ballet.
Michael Jackson(1958-2009) Michael Jackson is one of the popular dance icons and pop stars of the 1980s. He was not only a dancer but also a singer and a songwriter. He rose quickly to become one of the most famous dancers in history when he introduced the moonwalk during his performance of "Billie Jean" on the television special "Motown 25: Yesterday, Today, Forever" in 1983. This performance left a lasting impression and is considered a pivotal moment in dance and entertainment history.
Francisca Reyes Aquino(1899-1983) She was recognized as the Philippine National Artist for Dance in 1973. She is acknowledged as the Folk Dance Pioneer. This Bulakeña began her research on folk dances in the 1920s making trips to remote barrios in Central and Northern Luzon. Her research on the unrecorded forms of local celebration, ritual, and sport resulted in a 1926 thesis titled “Philippine Folk Dances and Games. She received the Republic Award of Merit for “outstanding contribution toward the advancement of Filipino culture.
Rudolf Nureyev(1938-1993) Rudolf Nureyev, (born March 17, 1938, Irkutsk, Russia, U.S.S.R.—died Jan. 6, 1993, Paris, Fr.), Russian ballet dancer. After studying ballet in Leningrad (1955–58), he joined the Kirov Ballet as a soloist. He defected during the company’s tour to Paris in 1961. Thereafter he danced as a guest artist with many companies, especially the Royal Ballet, where he regularly partnered Margot Fonteyn. His performances, combining an intensely romantic sensibility with stunning muscularity and technique, made him an international star.
